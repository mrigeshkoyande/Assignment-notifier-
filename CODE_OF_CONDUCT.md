# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The **Assignment Notifier & College Management Platform** project is committed to providing a welcoming, inspiring, and harassment-free community for all participants, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are dedicated to providing a positive experience for everyone who wants to participate constructively and respectfully.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Respect differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Being inclusive** — Welcome people from different backgrounds and perspectives
- **Being considerate** — Think about how your words and actions affect others
- **Being empathetic** — Show understanding and compassion toward community members
- **Being professional** — Keep discussions focused on the project and ideas
- **Being supportive** — Help others learn and grow
- **Using inclusive language** — Avoid assumptions and use welcoming terms
- **Providing constructive feedback** — Offer criticism that is helpful and respectful
- **Acknowledging mistakes** — Apologize sincerely when you cause harm

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

### Harassment
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuro(a)typicality, physical appearance, body size, age, race, or religion
- Unwelcome comments regarding a person's lifestyle choices and practices
- Deliberate misgendering or use of "dead" or rejected names
- Gratuitous or off-topic sexual images or behavior in spaces where they're not appropriate
- Physical contact and simulated physical contact (eg, textual descriptions like "*hug*" or "*backrub*") without consent or after a request to stop
- Threats of violence, both explicit and implicit
- Incitement of violence toward any individual, including encouraging a person to commit suicide or to engage in self-harm
- Deliberate intimidation
- Stalking or following
- Harassing photography or recording, including logging online activity for harassment purposes
- Sustained disruption of discussion
- Unwelcome sexual attention
- Pattern of inappropriate social contact, such as requesting/assuming inappropriate levels of intimacy with others
- Continued one-on-one communication after requests to cease
- Deliberate "outing" of any aspect of a person's identity without their consent except as necessary to protect vulnerable people from intentional abuse
- Publication of non-harassing private communication

### Exclusion
- Exclusion from spaces, activities, or opportunities based on protected characteristics
- Economic sanctions or boycotts based on discriminatory grounds

### Abuse of Process
- Making accusations that are dishonest or in bad faith
- Frivolous or vexatious complaints to avoid or deflect from legitimate accountability
- Retaliation against someone for reporting violations

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **Direct Communication** (if safe)
   - Contact the person privately to explain the impact of their behavior
   - Give them an opportunity to correct their actions

2. **Community Leadership**
   - Email: [mrigeshkoyande@gmail.com](mailto:mrigeshkoyande@gmail.com)
   - GitHub Issues: Use "Code of Conduct" label

3. **Include Details**
   - Description of the incident
   - Who was involved
   - When and where it occurred
   - Any relevant context or evidence

### Confidentiality

We take all reports seriously and treat them with appropriate confidentiality.

### Consequences

Community leaders will investigate complaints and determine appropriate responses:

1. **First Offense**
   - Verbal or written warning
   - Clarification of expectations
   - Opportunity to correct behavior

2. **Second Offense** (within 12 months)
   - Temporary suspension from community spaces
   - Restricted participation

3. **Third Offense** (within 12 months)
   - Permanent removal from the project
   - Ban from future activities

**Severe violations** (threats, harassment, abuse) may result in **immediate removal** regardless of history.

## Guidelines for Discussions

### Be Inclusive
- Use inclusive language and avoid assumptions
- Welcome people with different backgrounds and experiences
- Consider how your words might affect marginalized communities

### Be Constructive
- Provide feedback that helps, not hurts
- Explain the reasoning behind your suggestions
- Acknowledge good ideas and contributions

### Be Respectful
- Disagree respectfully without personal attacks
- Focus on ideas, not on the person
- Accept that others may have different perspectives

### Stay On Topic
- Keep discussions relevant to the project
- Use appropriate channels for different types of conversations
- Avoid derailing threads

### Use Clear Communication
- Be clear and direct in your writing
- Ask clarifying questions before making assumptions
- Explain technical concepts in accessible ways

## FAQ

### Q: What if I accidentally violate the Code of Conduct?

**A:** Everyone makes mistakes. If you unintentionally violate this policy:
1. Acknowledge the mistake
2. Apologize sincerely
3. Commit to change
4. Learn from the experience

Most violations are handled with education and correction, not punishment.

### Q: What if someone reports me unfairly?

**A:** We take false reports seriously. The investigation process includes:
- Hearing both sides of the story
- Gathering evidence
- Consulting with community leaders
- Making fair determinations
- Providing due process

### Q: Can violations be resolved informally?

**A:** Yes, many violations can be resolved through:
- Direct conversation between parties
- Mediation by community leaders
- Restorative justice approaches
- Understanding and mutual respect

### Q: What happens after I report something?

**A:** The reporting process:
1. Acknowledgment of receipt (within 24 hours)
2. Initial investigation (1-2 weeks)
3. Decision and response (1-2 weeks)
4. Follow-up (if needed)
